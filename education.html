Education

My Current and past education

I grew up in Sammamish, Washington, where I attended local schools from kindergarten through twelfth grade. My journey into higher education began early as I took college-level Spanish classes while still in high school, eager to challenge myself and enhance my language skills. This early exposure to college coursework was instrumental, although my senior year took an unexpected turn with the COVID-19 pandemic. As a result, I graduated from high school in my living room, a surreal experience that marked the end of my high school chapter in a most unusual way.

As I transitioned to college, my freshman year at Utah Valley University (UVU) was conducted entirely online due to the ongoing pandemic. This new virtual learning environment demanded flexibility and resilience, enabling me to continue my education despite the global challenges. After completing my first year at UVU, I took a break to serve a mission for the LDS Church, dedicating time to service and personal growth, which was profoundly rewarding and enlightening.

Upon returning from my mission, I transferred to Brigham Young University (BYU) and resumed my studies in the spring of 2023. Currently, as I finish up my sophomore year, I am preparing to apply to the BYU Business School and working towards earning a minor in Spanish. This decision to pursue a business degree, complemented by my continuing interest in Spanish, aligns with my career aspirations and my desire to integrate my passion for language with business acumen. I'm excited about the opportunities that lie ahead in this next phase at BYU, as I aim to build a strong foundation for my future professional endeavors.
